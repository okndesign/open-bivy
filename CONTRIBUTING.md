# Contributing

Thanks for being interested in this. This project is deliberately open to other perspectives — especially because it comes out of a design school project, not years of outdoor-gear experience.

## What's helpful

- **Test reports.** If you've built the prototype and tested it under different conditions (weather, ground, body size) — that's currently the biggest gap in the project, and your experience matters a lot.
- **Ideas and solutions for the open problems** (see [README](README.md#open-problems) and [status.md](docs/status.md)), in any form — a sketch, a rough prototype, or just a written idea.
- **Alternative approaches and redesigns.** If you look at this and think "I'd build this completely differently" — that's genuinely welcome. Open an issue, share a sketch, or just describe the idea. Different starting points are valuable, not a detour from "the" direction.
- **Photo/video documentation** of your own build attempts, including failed ones — that's just as valuable to others as a success.

## How to get involved

There's no required process to follow before contributing. Open an issue if you want to discuss an idea first, or just send a pull request if you already have something concrete — whatever fits how you like to work. Issue templates are there to help structure things, not to gatekeep.

If your idea touches on the [design principles](README.md#design-principles) (e.g. using adhesives or composite materials), it's worth a quick note on why — not because it's forbidden, but because it's a deliberate choice worth discussing openly so everyone understands the trade-off.

## License note for contributions

By submitting a pull request, you agree that your contribution will be published under the licenses used in this repo (CERN-OHL-S-2.0 for hardware designs, CC BY-SA 4.0 for documentation).

## Tone

Honest, constructive feedback is welcome — including "this won't work because...". Concrete and well-reasoned input helps everyone.

This project also follows our [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) — given the social context (homelessness), we ask for a respectful, thoughtful tone in all discussions.
